Smart strategies

Why this works

The math is simple. What stops people is not knowing the exact numbers. Now you do.

⚡

The cascading effect

When a debt is paid off, its minimum payment rolls into your extra payment automatically — accelerating every remaining debt. That's the snowball in action.

📉

Interest compounds against you

High-APR debt grows faster than you think. The avalanche method eliminates this growth at the source by targeting the most expensive balance first.

🧠

Psychology of the snowball

Research shows people stick to debt plans longer when they see early wins. The snowball method may cost slightly more interest, but completion rates are higher.

📅

Every month matters

Paying an extra $100/month can cut years off your debt timeline. The earlier you start, the more you save — time is your biggest asset here.

🎯

The hybrid approach

Can't decide? Hybrid weights 60% toward APR and 40% toward balance size — giving you most of the interest savings with some quick psychological wins.

💡

Track every payment

Use the Dashboard to log each payment you make. Watching real numbers drop is the most powerful motivation to keep going until you hit zero.

Common questions

Each month, interest accrues on every balance (APR ÷ 12), minimums are paid, then your extra payment cascades to debts in priority order. When a debt reaches $0, its minimum rolls into next month's extra. This compounds your speed — the cascade effect is real.

Avalanche directs extra money to the highest-APR debt first — mathematically optimal, you pay the least total interest. Snowball targets the smallest balance first — you get debt-free faster on individual accounts. Hybrid balances both factors.
